### **📌 SECTION 3: Differential Diagnosis – ALS Mimics & Overlapping Disorders (20 pages)**
#### **3A: ALS vs. Mimic Conditions (Key Differentiators)**  
📌 **Quick-Reference Table: ALS vs. Neuromuscular & Autoimmune Disorders.**  

| **Disease** | **Key Overlapping Symptoms** | **How ALS Differs** |
|------------|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Multifocal Motor Neuropathy (MMN)** | Asymmetric limb weakness, fasciculations | Anti-GM1 antibodies, No UMN involvement. |
| **Cervical Spondylotic Myelopathy (CSM)** | Limb weakness, spasticity, gait issues | MRI shows spinal cord compression. |
| **Myasthenia Gravis (MG)** | Bulbar symptoms, muscle weakness | Fluctuating weakness, fatiguability. |
| **Primary Lateral Sclerosis (PLS)** | Stiffness, spasticity | No LMN involvement, longer survival. |
| **Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA)** | LMN signs, progressive weakness | Genetic SMN1 mutation. |

#### **3B: The ALS "Negative Correlation" Phenomenon – Why ALS Stands Apart**
📌 **Unlike other neurodegenerative diseases, ALS does not correlate strongly with comorbid conditions.**  
📌 **This lack of co-occurring diseases can itself be a clue for diagnosis.**  
- **Why ALS patients rarely have cardiovascular disease.**  
- **Why ALS has no strong link to autoimmune disorders (unlike MS).**  
- **Why ALS does not correlate with psychiatric disorders (unlike Parkinson’s or FTD).**  
- **ALS patients have normal cognition (until later stages), unlike Alzheimer’s or Lewy Body Dementia.**
